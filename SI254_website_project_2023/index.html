What it's all about

WallStreet boasts a variety of topics but here are some we think you should know

Location

Wall Street is a historic and iconic street located in the Financial District of Lower Manhattan, New York City. Situated in the southern part of Manhattan, it stretches roughly eight blocks, running from Broadway to South Street on the East River.

Its name traces back to the 17th century when the Dutch built a wall as a defense against English invasion. Over the centuries, Wall Street has evolved into the epicenter of finance. Its location at the heart of the Financial District underscores its central role in the world of finance and makes it a focal point for economic activity and decision-making.

Big Names

The big names on Wall Street represent some of the most influential and significant financial institutions globally, with their origins dating back centuries. JPMorgan Chase & Co., Goldman Sachs Group Inc., Morgan Stanley, Bank of America Corporation, Citigroup Inc., and Wells Fargo & Co. are major banking and financial services corporations, providing a vast array of services ranging from retail and commercial banking to investment banking and asset management. Lastly, the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) stands as a monumental stock exchange, acting as a cornerstone of financial trading, where equities and securities are bought and sold, impacting economies and industries worldwide.

Interesting Facts

The iconic Charging Bull statue, located in Bowling Green Park, is a well-known Wall Street symbol representing bullish financial markets and optimism.

The street's history has witnessed numerous significant events, including the signing of the Buttonwood Agreement in 1792 under a buttonwood tree, which marked the founding of the New York Stock Exchange.

Wall Street is also home to Federal Hall, where George Washington took his oath of office as the first President of the United States.
